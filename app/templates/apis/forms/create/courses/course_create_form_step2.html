Course Sections Design

We believe in a revolutionary approach to education that transcends the traditional classroom model. Our courses are not structured around conventional subject matter like HTML, CSS, or JavaScript; instead, we immerse our students in real-world projects that serve as the backbone of their learning experience. Picture this: our instructors guide students through the creation of a management web application for a school, tackling challenges related to outdated average calculators and pitfalls in handling complex data. We don't start with the technology; we start with the problem. By introducing students to the inherent issues and inefficiencies in existing systems, we empower them to envision solutions. Only then do we delve into the world of web development or app, presenting a fully-fledged, feature-rich application that addresses the complexities of educational management. This unique philosophy ensures that our students not only master the technical skills required but, more importantly, develop a keen understanding of the problems they aim to solve. Our instructors play a pivotal role in guiding students to identify, comprehend, and ultimately overcome challenges, fostering a mindset that goes beyond coding to create impactful solutions in the real world. Warning

Our platform supports a variety of file types to enhance your course content. Please note the following maximum file sizes:

  • Images: 5MB
  • Electronic Circuit Designs: 15MB
  • 3D Models: 300MB
The file formats for the various types of files you might be dealing with are commonly as follows:
  • File Architecture (.zip, .tar, etc.): A compressed archive format is common for file architecture. Formats include .zip and .tar. You might compress a folder containing the necessary files and folders for the architecture.
  • Images (.jpg, .png, .gif, etc.): Common image formats include .jpg, .png, .gif, and others. The choice of format depends on the type of image and whether you need transparency or animation.
  • Electronic Circuit Designs (.pdf, .png, etc.): The format for electronic circuit designs can vary. You might use an image format like .png, or a document format like .pdf for more complex designs.
  • Complex 3D (.obj, .fbx, .gltf, etc.): For large 3D visualizations, formats like .obj, .fbx, and .gltf are common. These formats can store 3D models along with textures and animations.
Important: To ensure optimal performance and user experience, it's crucial to adhere to these size limits. If you have questions or need more details, consult our documentation for a comprehensive understanding of our file upload policy.
